A retreat built around the idea that the conditions for genuine human connection can be consciously designed — and that what happens in that space matters.

What It Is

Not yoga.
Not therapy.
Something harder to name.

The Connection Retreat draws from breathwork, yoga, acroyoga, tantra, Thai massage, authentic relating, meditation, ecstatic dance, and somatic practice — and weaves them into something that cannot be easily categorised.

The central question it holds is simple: what becomes possible when people feel genuinely safe with each other? The answer, in our experience, is something profound.

The Card System

Why every retreat
is different

Within the five-element framework, the specific workshops of each day are not pre-planned. At the start of each morning, a card is drawn from a deck. That card determines what the group will do together that day.

This is not improvisation for its own sake. It creates something important: genuine not-knowing. The facilitators don't know. The participants don't know. Everyone arrives into the day open — because that's the only option.

It also means that attending the retreat twice is attending two genuinely different retreats. The container holds. What fills it — emerges.

The Five Elements

The emotional arc
of the retreat

Each element holds a distinct emotional quality and shapes the texture of that day's experience. Together they form an arc — from arrival and grounding to integration and departure.

01

Earth

Grounding · Self-connection · Nervous system regulation

The first day is about arrival. Not just physically — but in the body, in the room, with this group of people. We slow down. We feel. We begin to listen to what is actually here, beneath the noise of ordinary life.

Practices drawn from this element tend towards somatic awareness, individual embodiment, and the quiet power of being still together. The nervous system finds its ground.

02

Water

Flow · Emotional openness · Partner connection · Authentic relating

We begin to turn towards each other. The quality of Water is one of flow — of meeting resistance with curiosity rather than force. Emotionally, it is about learning to feel without needing to fix.

Partner practices, authentic relating exercises, the practice of being truly listened to and truly hearing another person. Simple, and harder than it sounds.

03

Fire

Intensity · Transformation · Intimate pair work · Ecstatic dance

The midpoint of the retreat is its most intense. Fire asks us to meet the edges of what we can feel — and to discover that those edges are movable. SOMA breathwork, ecstatic dance, intimate pair work.

Not dramatic for its own sake. But genuinely activating. The kind of aliveness that is uncomfortable to approach and extraordinary once inside.

04

Air

Expression · Voice · Improvisation · Playfulness · Freedom

After the intensity of Fire, Air brings relief and joy. Voice work, acroyoga, improvisation, play. The discovery that the self which emerges when the pressure is released is often more authentic than the one we present to the world.

Laughter happens here. Real laughter. The kind that comes from shared aliveness rather than performance.

05

Space

Integration · Collective stillness · Meditation · Ritual · Closure

The final day is held gently. We integrate. What was opened is tended to. The group comes together for collective meditation, ritual, and ceremony. We hold what was touched — and we prepare to carry it forward.

Space is the element that makes room for all the others to be understood. Goodbyes happen here that are more honest than most hellos.

Daily Life

The rhythm
of a retreat day

Morning

Opening practice

Meditation, movement, or breathwork. A gentle arrival into the day — before the card is drawn.

Midday

Main session + nourishment

The central practice of the day, followed by communal meals prepared with care. Rest, integration, open time.

Evening

Evening session + sharing

A second practice session, followed by group sharing or free time. The day closes with intention.

Night

Rest & integration

What is experienced needs time to land. Evenings are gentle. Sleep is part of the practice.

Exact schedules vary by edition and are shaped in part by what the group needs.
