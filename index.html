The Story Behind It

Something essential
has gone missing.

We live in a time of constant contact and chronic disconnection. We scroll, we perform, we optimise — and somewhere along the way we lose the thread back to ourselves. To real touch. To honest conversation. To the felt sense of being here.

The Connection Retreat is not a yoga retreat. It is not a therapy weekend. It is not a wellness programme. It is something harder to name and more lasting in effect: a return to the fullness of human experience.

Five days. Five elements. A card deck drawn at random that makes every edition unique, unrepeatable, yours.

A dream. Lingering in the background for so long.

The Architecture of Experience

Five days.
Five elements.

Each day holds a distinct emotional quality. The practices within it are drawn from a card deck — making every retreat unique and unrepeatable.

Day One

Earth

Grounding · Self-connection · Nervous system

We arrive. We slow down. We feel the weight of our own bodies and learn to inhabit them again. Somatic awareness. Stillness. The ground beneath everything.

Day Two

Water

Flow · Emotional openness · Authentic relating

We soften. We begin to turn towards each other. Partner practices, authentic relating, the courage of real conversation. Emotion as information, not obstacle.

Day Three

Fire

Intensity · Transformation · Movement

We ignite. The midpoint of the retreat brings heat — breathwork, movement, intimate pair work. The edges of what we thought we could feel.

Day Four

Air

Expression · Voice · Play · Freedom

We lighten. Voice work, movement, improvisation, play. The self that emerges when the pressure is released. Laughter as medicine. Movement as joy.

Day Five

Space

Integration · Stillness · Ritual · Closure

We integrate. Collective meditation, ritual, the ceremony of closing. We hold what was opened. We carry it forward. We say goodbye knowing something has genuinely shifted.

Questions

Answered honestly,
without spin

Do I need experience in yoga, breathwork, or somatic practice?

No. The retreat is designed to be accessible to people at all levels of experience. What matters is not what you know but your willingness to be present and engaged. Many past participants have come with no background in any of the practices offered.

What does "tantra" mean in this context?

Nothing to be alarmed by. In the context of this retreat, tantra refers to the practice of full presence — meeting your experience, your sensations, and other people without suppressing or dramatising. It is about being alive to what is here, not about sexuality. Though it may touch on themes of intimacy, boundary, and authentic connection.

How many people attend each retreat?

Between 10 and 16 guests. This is intentional. The work requires real intimacy to function, and intimacy requires smallness. You will not be anonymous here.

Is every retreat different?

Yes. While the five-element framework remains consistent, the specific workshops within each day are drawn from a card deck at the start of that day. This means no two editions of the retreat are identical — including multiple editions in the same location.

Is this safe? What is your safeguarding approach?

Safety and consent are foundational to this work, not peripheral. We are in the process of finalising our consent and safeguarding framework before applications formally open. All practices are introduced with clear framing, and participation in any exercise is always an invitation, never a requirement. More detail will be available before any bookings are confirmed.
